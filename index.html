Welcome to Rock Valley College

So you’re thinking about attending RVC? We’re excited and eager to help you get to know our college! When you choose to get involved at RVC, you’ll guarantee yourself a complete college experience. Join a student organization. Attend some of our events. You’ll meet new people, become more creative, and develop critical thinking and leadership skills that will last a lifetime!

Rock Valley College is a comprehensive two-year community college in Rockford, Illinois, offering more than 100 courses for transfer, career programs and certificates. Rock Valley College is accredited by The Higher Learning Commission and is a member of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools. Rock Valley College is also recognized by the Illinois Board of Higher Education and by the Illinois Community College Board.

Whether you are interested in Career & Technical Education that is designed to be completed in two years or less, or you wish to start at RVC and transfer to another college, Rock Valley College has great options for you. Save big dollars by completing two years at RVC with a low tuition rate and transfer to the college or university of your choice.

Events

Rock Valley College hosts a variety events for both students and the community alike. Whether you enjoy sports, concerts, or live theater, RVC offers many options to entertain our extended RVC community.

Our music program offers a large selection of band and orchestral events. Tickets for all events are $5 for students/seniors and $10 for adults (except when noted as "free" or "freewill offering"). All proceeds benefit the RVC Music Student Scholarship Fund.

Our athletic programs also provide entertainment for the whole family. Besides the typical competitions with other schools, we offer more relaxed events such as the Annual Golf Outing, the All-Star Basketball Doubleheader, the Rumble in the Rock event, and the Taste the Hops events. Please visit our RVC sports website for more information for these events and ticket pricing.

Registration

As a new student, the transition to college can be overwhelming. It is critical to build a foundation for success during your first year of college. Let us help you with the process as you begin your journey here at Rock Valley College. To apply for admissions, apply online or pick up a paper form at the Information Center on the first floor of the Student Center on RVC's Main Campus. Once admitted, you have the option to apply for financial aid. Did you know half of RVC's students receive some sort of financial assistance (grants, scholarships, loans, or student employment)? We can help you fund your education.

All new students must meet placement requirements in reading, English and math. You can do that by taking our placement test or by submitting ACT or SAT scores or prior college transcripts if you have them. You will also meet with a helpful academic advisor to help make sure you are taking the right classes to meet your academic goals. Once you meet with your advisor, all you need to do is go online and sign up for your classes!
